You Can't Teach Art is the mantra that empowers artists everywhere to share and express their own creations or interpretations freely. Art is not to be mocked, but appreciated for what it really is. Join us in wearing the brand and share your experience with us and your friends by using our hashtag #YouCantTeachArt.

M I S S I O N

Art is unique. Everytime. Everything about it. Without the artists of this world there would be very little expression today. Artists empower the aesthetics that are significant to our everyday lives. In some way, we're all Artists. It's up to us to keep the light in the world. To each his own, fathoming what art is, who are we to judge what we did not create? Art is temporal, and therefore can't be taught. Don't fear sharing your work.

You Can't Teach Art.

Jason Rader Artist and Founder

A little bit about me, I've always been involved in art in some sort of way. I'm very much a hands on kinesthetic learner and art just has that way of taking hold of someone and teaching lessons we wouldn't find otherwise that enable us to learn and grow into who we become. I've been connected to art my entire life. Through coloring and scrillbing, music and the lyrics I would hear, martial arts training, as well as performing arts, I always found a sense of happiness between them. It's because of them, I am who I am today. I wouldn't change that for anything in the world. I am an Artist.

My artistic abilities developed a lot probably around th age of 4 when I began wrestling and training in other martial arts as well from a young age. I began acting as a summer activity when I was about 8 and, of course, always doodled. I didn't started seriously drawing until my senior year of high school, when I took Intro to Drawing 1, hoping for the easy A. I talked my way into taking independant art the next semester, which everyone knows is really a free period. Since then, I finally fully discovered my artistic abilities. I was just creating things for myself, and then other people wanted to buy them or asked if I could make something for them. I continued through college because I couldn't afford to just buy whatever cool artwork I wanted in my house, so I had to make all of it.

Being involved in so many different artistic fields just gave me so many differen't opportunities. I'm currently 23 years old and I am still all of these things ive described. I started You Can't Teach Art as a basic blog to just post my own personal art projects for my friends that were intersted. And then, when my hair got too long to keep out of my face, I started wearing a lot more hats. I looked at these solid black snap-backs on the shelf and thought, "that's just another canvas." I started getting a lot more traction and requests for customized hats with an unique design painted on them. Now with every project I produce I hope to inspire other artists to share and express their creations and their talents. It shouldn't be anything that we hold lightly.
